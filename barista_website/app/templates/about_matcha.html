What is Matcha?

Matcha is a finely ground powder made from specially grown and processed green tea leaves. Matcha is greatly known for its vibrant green color and unique flavor profile. It is traditionally used in Japanese tea ceremonies but has gained popularity globally for its versatility in culinary applications and potential health benefits.

Health benefits of matcha

Matcha has high concentration of antioxidants and other beneficial compounds. These include potential improvements in heart health, brain function, and weight management. Besides that, matcha also provides sustained energy boost without the jitters or crashes often associated with coffee, thanks to the combination of caffeine and L-theanine.

The L-theanine in matcha increases alpha wave activity in the brain, promoting relaxation and potentially reducing stress and anxiety.

Some studies even suggest that the EGCG in matcha may have cancer-preventing properties.

Important considerations

While matcha offers many potential benefits, it is essential to consume it in moderation, as excessive caffeince intake can lead to side effects such as anxiety, insomnia, or digestive issues.

It is also advised to avoid drinking matcha during iron-based meals because matcha is said to impede iron absorption properties in the body. Hence, it is best to consume matcha in between meals or meals which do not have high iron content.

Types of matcha

Ceremonial matcha

This is the highest grade of matcha powder. It is known for its rich umami flavor and it is designed mainly for traditional consumption (with only water). This means that it has fewer bitter nodes compared to other grades. However, it could still be used in latte creations, like those that I have been making!

Ceremonial matcha can be classified into several harvests or cultivars.

1st harvest/Sprint harvest

Harvested from the first flush of the youngest, most tender leaves, resulting in a delicate, sweet flavor.

Single cultivar

Made from leaves of the same harvest for a singular flavor profile.

Multiple/Blended cultivar

Made from different leaves to create a balanced flavor profile.

Premium grade/Second harvest

This grade contains a stronger flavor and is slightly bitter than ceremonial matcha, hence, it is optimal for lattes and everyday consumption.

Culinary grade

This is produced from later leaf harvests in the season. It contains harsher parts of the leaves, .e.g., the stems, hence, it is not as smooth in texture as other grades and may not mix well with water.

This grade has the strongest taste compared to other grades in terms of bitterness, so it is widely used in culinary/dessert and latte creations.

How to identify good matcha?

High quality matchas are vibrant green in color; lower-quality matchas are dull, yellowish in color.

Besides that, high quality matchas often originate from Japan, primarily Uji, Yame, Kagoshima, etc. High quality matchas usually froth well after whisking and hardly form clumps if whisked properly.

Tips of storing matcha

Always seal the matcha powder in an air-tight packaging/container after opening to minimise oxidation. Consume matcha within the recommended timeframe, at most 6 months. Last but not least, Avoid storing it directly under sunlit areas. It is best to store matcha in refrigerators/freezers for optimal freshness.
