Salted Coconut Cloud Ube Latte

Welcome to the recipe page for making salted coconut cloud ube latte!

What goes well with ube other than matcha? Coconut!!! Indulge in the traditional yet unique combination between the nutty flavor of ube (purple yam from the Philippines) with the richness of the coconut cream --- with a salty twist!

A salted coconut cream ube latte is typically made with ube halaya/paste or extract or coconut milk/cream. The beverage is often topped with a swirl of whipped coconut cream and may include other elements such as vanilla extract, sweetened condensed milk, or even toasted coconnut for added flavor and texture.

Ingredients

Coconut cream:

  • 2 tsp of oat milk or other plant-based milk of your choice
  • 1/4 cup / 60 ml of heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp of coconut cream
  • 1 pinch of salt

Ube latte:

  • 1/3 cup / 80 ml of oat milk or other plant-based milk of your choice
  • 1 cup / 1 large block of ice
  • 1/2 tbsp of ube paste/halaya
  • A few drops of ube syrup/extract

Equipment

  • Measuring spoons (optional)
  • Sieve
  • Bowl / Chawan
  • bamboo spoon / chashaku
  • Hand-held frother
  • Frothing pitcher / Cup or bowl for cream preparation (optional)

Recipe

Preparation Time:

5 mins

Cook time:

5 mins

Total time:

10 mins

Servings:

1 person

Base:

  1. Add ube paste/halaya into the cup for the drink.
  2. Add ice to the cup.
  3. Add milk to the cup.
  4. Add in ube syrup/extract.

Cream:

  1. In a frothing pitcher or a separate cup/bowl, add in heavy cream and the milk.
  2. Add in the coconut cream and salt.
  3. Whisk using a hand-held frother until the mixture is well-blended and creamy.

Final preparation:

  1. Pour the cream mixture into the cup for the drink. Tips on pouring: Gently pour the cream onto the tips of the ice to avoid mixing the cream with the latte. This is to create the floating effect. Pour in a circular motion.
  2. Enjoy!
